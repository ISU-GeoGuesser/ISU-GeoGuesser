# Product

## Register

brand

## Users
ISU students — especially incoming freshmen still learning campus — plus the IT 391
team demoing the project. They arrive on a phone or laptop wanting to jump straight
into a quick, competitive round, not read instructions.

## Product Purpose
ReggieGuessr is a GeoGuessr-style game for the Illinois State University campus:
players see a campus photo, drop a pin on a map, and score by how close they guess.
This page is the front door — it sets the tone and routes players into Single Player
or Multiplayer, with Login (university SSO) added later. Success = a player understands
what the game is in one glance and starts a round.

## Brand Personality
Collegiate, playful, competitive. Redbird pride without being a stuffy official .edu
page. Voice is confident and casual ("prove how well you really know the Quad").

## Anti-references
- Generic university homepage (dense nav, news carousels, stock smiling students).
- SaaS-cream landing pages with tiny tracked eyebrows over every section.
- Childish/cartoonish game UI — keep it grown and athletic, not Candy-Crush bright.

## Design Principles
- Let the campus photo be the design (full-bleed, GeoGuessr-style).
- One screen, one decision: play. Don't bury the play buttons.
- ISU red is the brand, not a garnish — it carries the primary action.
- Bare-bones now, but production-grade craft (a11y, motion, responsive) from the start.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
Target WCAG 2.1 AA: white-on-red primary button ≈ 5.6:1; edge scrim keeps body text
above 4.5:1 over the photo. Full `prefers-reduced-motion` fallback (no Ken Burns, no
entrance animation). Visible focus rings on every interactive element.
